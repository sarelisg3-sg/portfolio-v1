> **Prototype scope:** this redesign covers the resource search and download flow. Not all sections are interactive — some buttons and pages are outside the scope of this iteration.

## The problem

Le Point du FLE is a free directory of digital resources for learning and teaching French as a foreign language. The site gathered a large number of useful materials, but its architecture made them hard to find quickly.

Broad categories, lack of visual hierarchy, and the absence of clear filters forced users to explore too many paths before reaching a relevant resource.

This created **cognitive overload, wasted time, and uncertainty** when selecting materials by level, skill, or activity type.

## Target users

The redesign focused on French teachers and students who use digital resources to prepare lessons, practice independently, or find specific materials for their level.

The primary profile identified was **experienced French teachers** who need reliable, organized, ready-to-use resources without spending too much time searching.

## Redesign approach

The proposal reorganized the experience around three key needs:

1. **Search by level** — language learners think in terms of their CEFR level (A1–B2). The new homepage organizes content access directly by level.
2. **Filter by resource type** — combinable filter chips for grammar, vocabulary, pronunciation, printables, songs, and reading.
3. **Quickly evaluate whether a material fits** — cards with visible metadata and a resource preview before downloading.

A new information architecture, a clear search-and-download flow, and a centralized search bar accessible from the first screen were designed.

## Validation

The prototype was validated with **5 real users**.

- **100%** successfully found a resource on *passé composé* at A2 level
- **100%** identified how to download it

Based on the tests, **15 iterations** were made focused on improving level navigation, search result accuracy, system feedback, and user confidence when selecting a resource.

## Current status

The redesign is in progress. The homepage and level navigation are implemented in Next.js. Real content integration and the search results page are under development.
